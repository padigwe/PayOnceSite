1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that may be placed on your device when you visit a website. They are commonly used to:

  • make websites work properly
  • remember certain choices
  • improve security
  • understand how visitors use a website
  • support form handling and spam prevention

Similar technologies may also be used, including local storage, pixels, tags or other tools that store or access information on a user's device.

2. How Pay Once Sites Uses Cookies

Pay Once Sites aims to keep cookie use simple and limited. Our website may use strictly necessary cookies or similar technologies where required to:

  • operate the website
  • keep forms functioning securely
  • prevent spam or automated abuse
  • support basic site security
  • remember technical preferences necessary for site operation

Strictly necessary technologies are used because the website cannot function properly or securely without them.

3. Non-Essential Cookies

At the time of this policy, Pay Once Sites does not intentionally use non-essential advertising or marketing cookies on this website unless they have been clearly implemented and disclosed.

If we add analytics, advertising, retargeting or other non-essential tracking tools in the future, we will update this Cookie Policy and, where required, request appropriate consent before those technologies are used.

The website may use the following categories:

A. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These are required for the website to function, remain secure or process forms correctly. They may be used for form submission security, spam prevention, technical website operation and protecting the website from misuse. These cookies or technologies do not require consent where they are strictly necessary for the requested website service.

Our website contact form may be processed through third-party form handling and spam prevention services. These services may use technical mechanisms necessary to validate a form submission, prevent spam, reduce automated misuse and deliver the enquiry securely to us.

6. Managing Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • see what cookies are stored
  • block cookies
  • delete existing cookies
  • change cookie preferences for specific websites

Please note that blocking strictly necessary cookies or related technologies may affect how the website or contact form works.

7. Browser Controls

You can usually manage cookies in the settings of your browser, including browsers such as Google Chrome, Safari, Microsoft Edge and Mozilla Firefox. The exact steps vary depending on your browser and device.
